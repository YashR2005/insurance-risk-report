## ELSG-001 | electrical | Residual current device (RCD) protection of socket outlets
source: SS 638:2018, Chapter 41 (Protection against electric shock) — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Socket-outlet circuits rated up to 32 A and circuits supplying wet areas must be protected by a residual current device with a rated residual operating current not exceeding 30 mA, to provide additional protection against electric shock. RCDs must be tested periodically to confirm they trip within the required time.

## ELSG-002 | electrical | Overcurrent protection and distribution boards
source: SS 638:2018, Chapter 43 (Protection against overcurrent) — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Every circuit must be protected against overload and short-circuit by a protective device coordinated with the cable's current-carrying capacity. Distribution boards must be fitted with correctly rated circuit breakers, must not be overloaded, and final circuits must not be extended in a way that exceeds the design capacity of the protective device or conductor.

## ELSG-003 | electrical | Earthing and protective bonding
source: SS 638:2018, Chapter 54 (Earthing arrangements and protective conductors) — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Exposed conductive parts of Class I equipment must be connected to a protective earthing conductor, and main protective bonding must connect extraneous conductive parts to the main earthing terminal, so that a fault is cleared safely and dangerous touch voltages do not persist.

## ELSG-004 | electrical | Periodic inspection, testing and licensed work
source: SS 638:2018, Chapter 62 (Periodic inspection and testing); Electricity Act licensing of electrical workers — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Electrical installations must be periodically inspected and tested, with a notice stating the date of the last inspection and the recommended interval fixed at the origin of the installation. Installation, alteration and testing work must be carried out by a Licensed Electrical Worker, and low-voltage installations must comply with SS 638.

## ELSG-005 | electrical | Isolation and switching for maintenance
source: SS 638:2018, Chapter 46 (Isolation and switching) — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Every circuit must be provided with a means of isolation so that it can be safely switched off for maintenance, and a readily accessible main switch must allow the whole installation to be disconnected. Isolators must be capable of being secured against inadvertent re-energisation.

## ELSG-006 | electrical | Selection and erection of cables and wiring
source: SS 638:2018, Part 5 (Selection and erection of equipment), Chapter 52 (Wiring systems) — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Cables and wiring systems must be selected and installed with adequate current-carrying capacity for the load and protected against mechanical damage, heat and the external influences of their location. Cables must be properly supported and not run where they can be damaged or overheat.

## ELSG-007 | electrical | Protection in bathrooms and wet locations
source: SS 638:2018, Part 7 (Special installations or locations) — https://www.ema.gov.sg/regulations-licences/regulations/standards-guidelines/singapore-standards-and-technical-references
Installations in bathrooms, wet and damp locations must have additional protection and use equipment with a degree of ingress protection (IP rating) suitable for the zone, with socket outlets and accessories restricted or protected to reduce the risk of electric shock in the presence of water.
