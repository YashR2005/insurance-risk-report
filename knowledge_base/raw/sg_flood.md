## FLSG-001 | flood | Minimum platform level for buildings
source: PUB Code of Practice on Surface Water Drainage (minimum platform level) — https://www.pub.gov.sg/Public/KeyInitiatives/Flood-Resilience/Stormwater-Management
New developments must be built to a minimum platform level — raised to 4 metres Singapore Height Datum (SHD) in flood-prone catchments — so that the finished floor sits above the design flood level and the building is protected from surface-water flooding.

## FLSG-002 | flood | Basement crest levels and flood barriers
source: PUB Code of Practice on Surface Water Drainage (crest level / flood protection) — https://www.pub.gov.sg/Public/KeyInitiatives/Flood-Resilience/Stormwater-Management
Entrances, exits and openings to basements and underground structures must be provided with a crest level set above the minimum platform level (raised to 300 mm above platform level), and flood-protection measures such as flood barriers must be installed and maintained to prevent ingress of floodwater.

## FLSG-003 | flood | On-site detention and drainage maintenance
source: PUB Code of Practice on Surface Water Drainage (source solutions / drainage) — https://www.pub.gov.sg/Public/KeyInitiatives/Flood-Resilience/Stormwater-Management
Developments must manage stormwater at source, including on-site detention tanks to attenuate peak run-off, and internal drains, gratings and discharge points must be kept clear and maintained so that drainage capacity is not reduced by silt or debris.

## FLSG-004 | flood | Protection of critical M&E equipment
source: PUB Code of Practice on Surface Water Drainage, Clause 2.5 (Critical M&E Equipment) — https://www.pub.gov.sg/Public/KeyInitiatives/Flood-Resilience/Stormwater-Management
Critical mechanical and electrical equipment — such as main switch rooms, generators, pumps and lift motor rooms — must be located above the minimum platform / flood level or otherwise protected from flooding, so that essential services are not disabled during a flood event.

## FLSG-005 | flood | ABC Waters design features
source: PUB Code of Practice on Surface Water Drainage, ABC Waters Design Features — https://www.pub.gov.sg/Public/KeyInitiatives/Flood-Resilience/Stormwater-Management
Developments should integrate ABC (Active, Beautiful, Clean) Waters design features such as bioretention basins, rain gardens and detention storage to slow and treat stormwater at source, reducing peak run-off into the public drainage system and improving water quality.

## FLSG-006 | flood | Drainage reserves and outfall structures
source: PUB Code of Practice on Surface Water Drainage (drainage reserve / outfall structures) — https://www.pub.gov.sg/Public/KeyInitiatives/Flood-Resilience/Stormwater-Management
Drainage reserves must be kept clear of unauthorised structures and obstruction to allow maintenance access, and outfall structures discharging to public drains or the sea must be properly designed and maintained so that flow is not impeded and bank or channel erosion is prevented.
